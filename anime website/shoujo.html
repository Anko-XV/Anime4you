shoujo

Shōjo manga (少女漫画, lit. "girls' comics", also romanized as shojo or shoujo) is a genre of Japanese editorial comics aimed at an audience of adolescent girls and young adult women. That is, with shōnen manga (targeted at teenagers), seinen manga (targeted at youth and adult men) and josei manga (targeted at adult women), one of the main editorial genres of manga. Shōjo manga are traditionally published in magazines dedicated to manga, often specializing in a particular age group or narrative genre. Shōjo manga emerged from Japanese girls' culture in the early 20th century, mainly as shōjo shōsetsu (girls' prose novels) and jojōga (lyrical paintings). Shoujo manga was first published in general magazines for teenagers in the early 1900s and entered a period of creative growth starting in the 1950s when it began to be formalized as a genre. separate manga. Although the genre was initially dominated by male comic artists, the emergence and eventual dominance of female artists starting in the 1960s and 1970s led to a period of remarkable creative innovation. tell and develop more graphically and thematically complex stories. Since the 1980s, the category has continued to expand stylistically while branching out into different and overlapping subgenres. Strictly speaking, shoujo manga does not refer to a specific style or genre but only to its target demographic. Although there are certain aesthetic, visual, and narrative conventions associated with shoujo manga, these conventions have changed and evolved over time, and none are entirely specific to shoujo manga. . However, a number of concepts and themes are now commonly associated with shoujo manga, both visual (non-rigid composition, eye for detail) and narrative (emphasis on relationships and emotions). human emotions (characters challenge traditional roles and stereotypes surrounding gender and emotions).

sailor moon

One day, Usagi Tsukino, a junior high school student in Juban, Tokyo, befriends Luna, a talking black cat, and receives a magical brooch that allows her to transform into Sailor Moon. A Guardian destined to save the Earth from the forces of evil. Luna and Usagi team up with other Sailor Senshi to search for the Princess and the Silver Crystal. They meet Ami Mizuno, a hard worker who has awakened as Sailor Mercury. Rei Hino, a shrine maiden at a local shrine, awakens as Sailor Mars. Makoto Kino is a tall and strong transfer student who awakens as Sailor Jupiter. Minako Aino is a young aspiring idol who woke up a few months ago as Sailor Venus with her talking cat friend Artemis. They also befriend Mamoru Chiba, a high school student who sometimes helps them as Tuxedo Mask.

inuyasha

In 1496 Japan, humans and demons battle over the Shikon JewelKikyo, the priestess who keeps the Shikon Jewel, is in love with the half-demon Inuyasha. Inuyasha steals the Shikon Jewel, but the dying Kikyo pins Inuyasha to a tree with a sacred arrow. The Shikon Jewel manifests from within the body of Kagome, who is Kikyo's reincarnation, and she desperately frees Inuyasha from the tree to kill the centipede demon. Kagome inadvertently shatters the Shikon Jewel into many shards with an arrow, and they scatter across Japan and into the possession of various demons and humans.

ouran high school host club

The comedy revolves around the adventures of Haruhi Fujioka, a scholarship student at Ouran Academy, a prestigious private school for wealthy children in Tokyo's Bunkyo Ward. Looking for a quiet place to study, Haruhi stumbles upon the abandoned Third Music Room, where the Host Club, a group of six male students, gathers to entertain ``female regulars''. When they first meet her, Haruhi accidentally breaks an antique vase worth 8 million yen and ends up having to pay off her debt by running errands for her club.

banana fish

Banana Fish is set in the United States during the mid-1980s, primarily in New York. Ash Lynx, a 17-year-old street gang leader, is cared for by his older brother Griffin, a veteran of the Vietnam War. Griffin had been in a vegetative state since a combat incident in which he opened fire on his unit and uttered the word "banana fish". One night, Ash witnessed two gang members kill a man who told them to "find some banana fish" before dying. The two gang members told Ash that they were acting on orders from Dino Golzine, the leader of the Corsican mafia in New York. A former Golzine enforcer and child sex slave, Ash was groomed from a young age to become the ultimate successor to his criminal organization.
